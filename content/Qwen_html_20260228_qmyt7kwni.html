Frequently Asked Questions

What is digital citizenship?

Digital citizenship refers to the responsible, ethical, and safe use of technology. It includes understanding your rights and responsibilities online, protecting your privacy, respecting others, and contributing positively to digital communities.

How can I protect my privacy online?

Protect your privacy by using strong, unique passwords, enabling two-factor authentication, adjusting privacy settings on social media, being cautious about sharing personal information, and using secure connections (HTTPS).

What should I do if I experience cyberbullying?

If you experience cyberbullying: 1) Don't respond to the bully, 2) Save evidence (screenshots), 3) Block the person, 4) Report to the platform, 5) Tell a trusted adult or authority, 6) Seek support from friends or counselors.

How do I identify fake news online?

To identify fake news: 1) Check the source credibility, 2) Look for multiple sources confirming the story, 3) Check the date, 4) Look for evidence and citations, 5) Be wary of sensational headlines, 6) Use fact-checking websites.

What is a digital footprint?

Your digital footprint is the trail of data you leave behind when using the internet. This includes websites you visit, posts you make, photos you share, and information you submit. It can be permanent, so think before you post!

How can I create a strong password?

Create strong passwords by: 1) Using at least 12 characters, 2) Mixing uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols, 3) Avoiding common words or personal info, 4) Using a password manager, 5) Never reusing passwords across accounts.
